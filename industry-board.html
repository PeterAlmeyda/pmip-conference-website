About the Industry Advisory Board

The PMiP Industry Advisory Board is a distinguished group of project management professionals, academics, and industry leaders who guide the strategic direction of the annual PMiP Conference. Established to ensure the conference remains grounded in real-world practice and aligned with the evolving demands of the profession, the board brings together voices from across sectors and disciplines.

Their collective expertise shapes everything from the conference theme and keynote topics to the abstract review process and program curation. For 20 years, the board's involvement has been central to PMiP's reputation as one of the most credible and practitioner-focused PM events in the region.

The Board's Role & Responsibilities

The Industry Advisory Board serves as the intellectual and strategic backbone of the PMiP Conference. Members contribute in the following ways:

  • Theme Advisory, Board members identify emerging trends and pain points in their respective industries, which directly inform the conference theme and sub-tracks each year.
  • Abstract Review, Board members participate in evaluating submitted abstracts and papers, ensuring accepted presentations meet a high bar for relevance and rigor.
  • Keynote Curation, The board advises on keynote speaker selection, helping identify voices that are both authoritative and timely.
  • Program Endorsement, Board members review the finalized program before publication to confirm it reflects current industry needs.
  • Community Engagement, Members serve as ambassadors within their professional networks, expanding the conference's reach and credibility.
  • Post-Conference Feedback, Board members provide structured feedback after the event to inform future iterations.

How the Board Shapes the Program

The connection between the board's input and what attendees experience on conference day is direct and intentional. Each cycle begins with a board-led environmental scan, members share what they are seeing in their organizations, what questions their teams are wrestling with, and where they believe the profession is underprepared.

For PMiP 2026, that process surfaced three dominant themes that now anchor the program: the accelerating integration of AI tools into project delivery, the leadership and change management challenges that come with it, and the strategic alignment questions facing PMOs navigating organizational transformation.

The three tracks, Leadership, Technology, and Strategy, were not chosen arbitrarily. They map directly to the domains where board members reported the most urgency. Every accepted paper, every panel, and every keynote was evaluated against the question: does this help a practitioner solve a real problem, today? That standard is the board's most lasting contribution to PMiP.
