Welcome to PMiP

Welcome to the official website of the Project Management in Practice (PMiP) Conference at Boston University's Metropolitan College. This platform serves as the central hub for conference information, attendee communication, and stakeholder engagement.

As interest in project management continues to grow across industries — including technology, healthcare, construction, finance, and higher education — conferences like PMiP have become essential spaces for connecting students, educators, and industry professionals. This website was created to give the PMiP Conference the centralized digital presence it deserves.

About the Conference

The Project Management in Practice Conference is an annual event hosted by the Department of Administrative Sciences at Boston University's Metropolitan College. The conference provides a dedicated space for project management professionals, educators, and students to share practical knowledge, explore industry trends, present research, and exchange real-world experiences.

PMiP bridges the gap between academic rigor and professional practice. Students seeking internships and career opportunities connect directly with seasoned professionals, while industry experts engage with emerging talent and the latest academic thinking in the PM field. The result is a uniquely collaborative environment that benefits every stakeholder in attendance.

This year's conference marks a historic milestone — the 20th Anniversary Vanguard Forum — bringing together alumni from every graduating cohort since 2006 alongside the PMiP Industry Board for a once-in-a-generation strategic gathering.

What to Expect

The PMiP Conference delivers a full day of structured programming including keynote addresses, panel discussions, breakout workshops, and networking opportunities — all designed to connect practical field experience with forward-looking industry strategy.

Conference Sessions — Six signature sessions structured around key themes in project management, from founding principles to emerging AI-driven frameworks and methodologies.

Distinguished Speakers — Keynote presentations and panel discussions led by industry leaders, PMiP alumni, and academic professionals with decades of combined experience.

Industry Board Dialogue — Direct engagement with the PMiP Industry Board, chaired by Mr. Wasim Al-Rashid, providing macro-level strategic oversight and industry perspective.

Networking Reception — A dedicated post-conference reception open to all registered attendees, providing space for informal connection and professional relationship-building.

About Boston University Metropolitan College

For over half a century, Boston University's Metropolitan College (MET) has brought the scholarly rigor and resources of an internationally respected research university to busy, working adults via innovative, part-time programs. As one of the 17 degree-granting bodies at BU, MET offers more than 80 graduate and undergraduate degree and certificate programs — evenings on campus, online, and in blended formats designed to meet the needs of both the local and global professional community.

A laboratory and incubator for new programs, pedagogy, and educational technologies, MET ensures that students have access to cutting-edge facilities and the latest learning tools — as well as the opportunity to build networks with classmates and industry peers. MET's online master's degrees in management are ranked #12 in the nation by U.S. News & World Report for 2026.

About the Department of Administrative Sciences

BU MET's Department of Administrative Sciences offers rigorous management programs that embrace innovation, evolving technology, and the globally interconnected nature of today's business sectors. Available in flexible formats — including part time and full time, online, and evenings on campus — the department's graduate degree and certificate programs are continuously reviewed and updated to integrate emerging specialties within industry.

The PMiP Conference is hosted and organized under the Department of Administrative Sciences, reflecting the department's commitment to connecting academic excellence with real-world professional practice in the field of project management.
